Three-Body Problem

A stable solution where three bodies chase each other in a figure-8 orbit.

The Physics Behind It

Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

Every particle attracts every other particle with a force directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between their centers.

Velocity Verlet Integration

We use a numerical method called Velocity Verlet to calculate the positions and velocities at each time step. This method is symplectic, meaning it conserves energy better than simpler methods, making orbits like the Figure-8 stable.

The Three-Body Problem

Unlike the two-body problem, the three-body problem generally has no closed-form solution. It is often chaotic, meaning small changes in initial conditions lead to vastly different outcomes (Butterfly Effect).
